EcoMap
I started an environmental project with a friend from North Carolina. I witnessed the negative impacts of pollution through trips to India but realized I hadn’t taken action against the prevalent environmental threat. So I embarked on a project with my friends to create an interactive map that raises awareness of environmental problems around the world. Building on the work that existing organizations were doing, we devised an online platform to unite those organizations in a single space. Using HTML 5 and CSS, we designed a website to house our interactive map. We used the diagram developing platform MapHub to create our map. When a user clicks on an area, s/he will understand the environmental concern in that region, and the map will connect that individual to a nonprofit that is attempting to counter the problem. The individual can then easily donate to the nonprofit. Some of the environmental concerns that are highlighted on the map are the melting of ice caps, destruction of coral reefs, endangered species, and loss of animal habitats.
NoQNow
I took part in an innovative project that I worked on with a team in India. Our concept is an online platform (website) for individuals to shop using a token system to maintain social distancing when going grocery shopping. India has a huge population density, and the token system is a form of scheduling that allows people to sign up for a specific time slot to shop. We successfully implemented the system in several grocery stores in India. [https://www.issuewire.com/indian-techie-develops-app-for-social-distancing-for-grocery-stores-1665103202697196 ] My idea was to help bring this similar platform to the US. I targeted small local businesses and clinics to help them maintain social distancing and, specifically, to reduce wait times in waiting rooms. I also reached out to local grocery stores, restaurants, and other small businesses and found that most small businesses in the US weren’t facing social distancing issues/waiting issues in their stores. Based on this research, I did not see the applicability of the token model in the local North Texas market.
Chatbot Developer for NonProfits
After completing my internship at ValueLabs, I wanted to put my skills to work while helping different local non-profit organizations by developing chatbots for their sites. I first emailed major global organizations and learned that they were more likely to not require a chatbot. I then decided to target local non-profits whose websites were less organized and would most benefit from a chatbot that helps users navigate through the site. Through this initiative, I gained insight on how to pitch technology and the technology consulting process. I also helped the organizations come up with potential uses for the chatbot. Some organizations were very much open to the idea but didn’t know exactly why they needed the bot. I guided them in coming up with these uses by asking them to provide FAQs, basic details they want people to know, and how volunteers could help their organization.
Coding Lead/Builder, First Tech Challenge (FTC) Robotics Team Overdrive!
During the 2020-2021 season, I participated in the Advanced Functions subteam where we designed the functions that focus on enhancing the performance of our robot in aspects other than the main game objectives such as applying computer vision for the robot to recognize game elements, odometry to help the robot track its location during the autonomous period, and a wheel intake to help the bot take in-game elements faster. As an Advanced Function Team member, we designed our hardware using the Fusion 360 CAD Software and code our hardware using the Android Studio IDE and Java Programming. We also implemented the Roadrunner Java library to standardize our robot’s movement during its autonomous period. Moreover, we implemented the OpenCV library in order to leverage computer vision for the robot to detect objects by itself during the game. As a Coding Lead, I ensure that there is consistency among the code across the different sub-teams that are working on various parts of the robot.
